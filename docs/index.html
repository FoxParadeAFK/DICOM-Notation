DICOM (Digital Imaging and Communications In Medicine) is a standard designed for the storage and exchange of medical images. They have been a central pillar to the evolution of modern radiology imaging. You may be aware of its applications in computed tomography (CT), magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), among others.

DICOM was a topic I stumbled upon whilst researching possible final-year project ideas. In this article, I'll cover the basics of DICOM, examples of deciphered information, and renders of images from within.

This project is deeply inspired by Sebastian Lague's "Coding Adventures: Rendering Text". In the video, he showcases his journey parsing TrueType files, a font standard designed by Apple. Lague used C# and Unity to parse and render, although in my case, I'll use GDScript and Godot instead. I don't believe the substitution should create any unfavourable side effects. Hopefully anyway.
